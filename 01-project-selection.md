# Phase 1: Project Selection

## Sustainability gap analysis

The strongest internship project should avoid common topics and focus on a real problem where AI adds measurable value. Current overlooked sustainability gaps include climate adaptation for vulnerable workers, invisible urban heat exposure, digital repair and reuse, household chemical leakage, cooling inequality, safe low-carbon mobility for people with disabilities, and localized resilience planning.

The selected problem should be rare enough to stand out, but realistic enough for a student demo. The best fit is a project that can use AI for classification, retrieval, prediction, and decision support without needing expensive hardware.

## Best project selected

**ThermaRoute AI: Thermal-Equity Copilot for Climate-Resilient Campuses**

ThermaRoute AI scored highest because it balances originality, practical demo creation, AI depth, measurable sustainability value, and social impact. It is unusual compared with common internship ideas, yet evaluators can immediately understand its relevance. Extreme heat is a major climate adaptation challenge, and campuses are a realistic testbed because they include students, staff, visitors, outdoor workers, security guards, canteen workers, and maintenance teams.

The project is startup-grade because it has a clear product surface: heat risk assessment, route planning, rest and hydration recommendations, RAG-backed safety guidance, hotspot reports, and infrastructure planning. It is research-inspired because it uses official heat-health and urban heat island evidence. It is feasible because all data can be mocked for a prototype while the architecture supports real weather, GIS, and sensor integrations later.
