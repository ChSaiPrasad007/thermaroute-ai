# LinkedIn Project Post

I am excited to share my AI for Sustainability project: **ThermaRoute AI**, a thermal-equity copilot for climate-resilient campuses.

Instead of choosing a common topic like generic waste segregation or a basic carbon calculator, I focused on an overlooked sustainability challenge: hyperlocal heat exposure. A weather app may show one city temperature, but a student walking across an asphalt gate, a security guard standing near a road, or a maintenance worker on a metal rooftop can face very different heat risks.

ThermaRoute AI helps campuses:

- assess heat risk by location, surface, shade, humidity, task, and user group;
- retrieve trusted heat-health guidance using RAG;
- generate safer route, rest, hydration, and schedule recommendations;
- collect anonymous heat hotspot reports;
- prioritize shade, water, tree cover, cool surfaces, and climate adaptation actions.

The project is aligned with SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities, with secondary alignment to SDG 3 and SDG 13. I built the prototype with a React frontend, FastAPI backend, SQLite reporting, a deterministic AI fallback, and an OpenAI-compatible adapter for future IBM Granite integration.

This project helped me understand how AI can support sustainability when it is practical, responsible, inclusive, and grounded in real community needs.
